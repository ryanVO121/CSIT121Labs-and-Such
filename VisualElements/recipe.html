The Best Choclate Mousse

By The Food Network. Close your eyes and take a bite -- you'll be transported to a 4-star French restaurant.

.box{ width: 600px; height: 200px ; backgroun: linear-gradient(red, purple); }

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cups cold heavy cream
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder
  • 1 traspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 4 ounces bittersweet choclate, chopped
  • 4 ounces semisweet choclate, chopped
Directions:
  1. Whipe 1 cup of heavy cream into soft peaks into a bowl and set it aside.
  2. Add the egg, egg yolks, sugar, espresso powder, vanilla extract, salt and 1 tablespoon water to a seperate bowl and whisk until combined.
  3. Set the bowl over a medium so=aucepan of simmering waterand whisk it constantly until the mixture is pale, hot, and nearly doubled in volume four around 5 minutes, scraping down teh bowl with a spatula if necessary.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and continue to whisk until it's cooled.
  5. Put the choclate ina another bowl and set it over the simmering saucepan. Stir occasional with a rubber spatula until the chocolate is completely melted.
  6. remove the choclate from the heat and stir for around 3 minutes till slighlty cooled.
  7. Whisk the egg mixture into the choclate mixture in 3 seperate additions.
  8. Using a rubber spatula, fold teh cream into teh choclate until fully mixed.
  9. Divide teh choclate mousse into 4 unce cups and chill until firm for around 1 hour.
  10. Whipe the remaining 3/4 cup of heavy cream into peaks and top each mousse cup with the cream.
